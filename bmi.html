What your BMI result means

A BMI range of 18.5 to 24.9 is considered a “healthy weight.” Maintaining a healthy weight may lower your chances of experiencing health issues later on, such as obesity and type 2 diabetes. Aim for a nutritious diet with reduced sugar and saturated fat, and ensure you get regular physical activity, ideally about 30 minutes daily for five days a week.

Healthy eating

Eat a diet with vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, whole grains. Avoid processed food and excess sugars.

Regular exercise

Physical activity helps control weight, reduce health risks, and strengthen your body.

Adequate sleep

Get 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to boost mental and physical health.

Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is a useful indicator of healthy weight, it doesn't consider muscle, age, gender, or body type.

Gender

Men and women store fat differently. Women naturally carry more body fat than men.

Age

Aging causes changes in body composition — muscle mass decreases, fat often increases.

Muscle

Muscular individuals may appear overweight by BMI, despite being fit and healthy.
